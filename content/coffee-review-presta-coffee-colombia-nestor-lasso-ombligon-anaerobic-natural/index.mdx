## The Science of the Cup: Anaerobic Natural Processing

The processing behind this coffee is as meticulous as you'd expect from a producer of Nestor's caliber. This lot underwent a carefully controlled Anaerobic Natural process that reads more like a chemistry experiment than traditional coffee processing:

Cherries were hand-selected at optimal ripeness, measured between 21‚Äì24¬∞ Brix (that's seriously sweet fruit). They were placed in open plastic tanks for a 48-hour oxidation phase, during which the naturally released coffee must was constantly recirculated and monitored to maintain ideal fermentation conditions ‚Äî roughly 19¬∞ Brix and a pH above 5.

After oxidation, the cherries were submerged in cold water to separate any underripe or underdeveloped fruit (only the densest, fully ripe cherries make the cut). Then comes a fascinating step: a 50¬∞C warm water rinse that creates a controlled thermal shock, loosening the molecular structure of the fruit and kickstarting a more active fermentation phase.

Finally, the cherries were sealed in airtight plastic tanks for anaerobic fermentation ‚Äî a completely oxygen-free environment where complex microbial activity develops the unique flavor characteristics that make this coffee sing. The cherries are then dried in their full fruit form, locking in all that developed complexity.

It's this level of precision and care that separates world-class lots from merely good ones. Every step has been thought through, tested, and refined.

## The Region: Huila's Rising Star

Huila has quietly become one of the most exciting coffee-producing regions on the planet. Centered around Pitalito, where Finca El Diviso is located, the region sits where the Central and Eastern ranges of the Andes converge ‚Äî creating a unique microclimate that's ideal for growing exceptional coffee.

What makes Huila's recent rise so compelling is that it's been driven by the producers themselves. Predominantly smallholder farms, the region has made a concerted effort over the past decade to shift toward specialty coffee production. Selective manual harvesting, attentive processing, and meticulous post-harvest sorting have all contributed to Huila's growing recognition on the global stage.
